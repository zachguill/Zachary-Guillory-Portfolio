Footwear

Footwear is perhaps the most important piece of gear you must choose when wanting to go on a hike. Urban hikes only require a good pair of running shows. Nature hikes, depending on the difficulty, can require running shoes all the way up to mountaineering boots. Below is a table of footwear trusted by our contributors ranked by cost.

Type Brand Shoe Cost
Running Shoes Brooks Adrenaline GTS $90
Hiking Boots Salomon X Ultra 4 Mid $160
Mountaineering Boots Asolo Fugitive GTX $300

Running Shoes

Running shoes are the most basic footwear one should wear on a hike. Comfortable, simple, and low-cost, these shoes are perfect for urban hikes or low-difficulty nature hikes. Most of us already own a pair, but if you do not, the Brooks Adrenaline is a good choice.

Hiking Boots

Hiking boots are the next step up in ruggedness and cost. A good pair of hiking boots can last a few years of rough use. This choice is at home on moderate hiking trails that require better traction than running shoes and ankle support.

Mountaineering Boots

Mountaineering boots are the most rugged and the most expensive of the footwear options. The boots are only needed for the most difficult of hiking trails. They can usually accept spikes and have some kind of rock guard on the toe.
