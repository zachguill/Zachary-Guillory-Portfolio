Freedom Trail

One of our nation's premier urban hikes is the Freedom Trail located in Boston, Massachusetts. The trail functions as a walking tour for many of the important Revolutionary War sites in Boston.

Difficulty

At only two and a half miles in length and paved all the way, the Freedom Trail is an experience open to all age groups and skill levels. Additionally, there are many places along the trail to rest or get refreshments. Hiking Simple rates this trail as easy.

Finding the Trailhead

The Freedom Trail starts at Boston Common. Boston Common is easily accessed by public transportation.

The Hike

After Starting in Boston Common, visitors will follow the trail to the Massachusetts State House, known for its iconic gold dome. Walking along the trail will take walkers past many historic churches and burial sites, each of which can be stopped at for more historical information. The First Iconic historical building one will reach is the Old State House, which survived the Revolutionary War intact.

After seeing the Old State House, visitors will pass the site of the Boston Massacre on their way to Faneuil Hall, a great place to stop for lunch. Continuing on one will pass Paul Revere's house and reach Old North Church.

The next leg of the trail is the longest without a historical site, but by no means should you skip the rest. After crossing the Charles River, one will be able to visit the USS Constitution, the oldest commissioned naval vessel in the world.

The last stop on the trail is the Bunker Hill Memorial. Visitors can wall to the top for a view of downtown Boston, but the steps are a difficult walk so those who are out of shape or have a fear of heights should just enjoy the view from the bottom.
