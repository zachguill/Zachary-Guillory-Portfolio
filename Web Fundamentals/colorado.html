Almagre Mountain

Almagre Mountain is the second tallest peak in the Pikes Peak area. It is noteworthy for its stunning views of Pike Peak.

Difficulty

Almagre Mountain, while not one of Colorado's famous 14ers, is by no means an easy hike. With six total miles and a 2,000 ft change in elevation, this hike should only be attempted by hikers with some mountain experience. Hiking Simple rates this trail as moderate.

Finding the Trailhead

The trailhead is located deep in the national forest. To find it first start in Criple Creek and take the service road into the national forest. The parking area is small so it is recommended to arrive early in the day.

The Hike

The first mile or so of the trail is relatively steep, and is one of the more challenging parts of the hike, but as you make your way up the trail you begin to see stunning views of Pikes Peak.

As you continue up the trail there are many places to stop and rest. At many of these rest points views of the Sawatch Range and alpine lakes are visible.

Finally you will cross the tree line and near the peak of Almagre Mountain. From the peak, looking north, is one of the best views you can find of Pikes Peak, America's mountain.
