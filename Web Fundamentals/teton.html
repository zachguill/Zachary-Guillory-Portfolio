Grand Tetons

Our Grand Teton hike recommendation is the trail to Lake Solitude. This Trail is noted for its views of Grand Teton, an alpine lake, and chances to see wildlife.

Difficulty

At almost 17 miles round trip and 2,000 feet of elevation change this trail is not for inexperienced hikers. Additionally, due to its northern latitude, there may be snow on the trail well into the summer. Hikers must also take caution as wildlife like moose and bears will be present on the trail. It is recommended to hike in pairs and carry bear spray. Hiking Simple rates this trail as difficult.

Finding the Trailhead

The trailhead starts at Jenny Lake Visitor Center which has limited parking for a national park. In the summer if one wants to skip a few miles, a ferry can transport you across the lake. Those who do not take the ferry will walk about a mile around Jenny Lake.

The Trail

The trail starts with a not-too-difficult hike along Cascade Creek for approximately four miles. Here, visitors will be greeted by a rushing mountain stream and may stumble upon either moose or brown bear.

It is important not to get too close to wildlife. If one is on the trail, keep your distance and wait for it to pass. Farther along the trail you will reach a point where the trail and Cascade Creek split in a y intersection. Here, take the right path. A little farther up the trial, you will reach your first rock fall. Now that you are clear of the trees, it is a good time to turn around and catch a view of the mountains.

Here the trail gets more difficult. The trail gets steeper and this is where, in the early season, you will begin to encounter snow. As you reach the top of the trail you will be greeted by Lake Solitude.
